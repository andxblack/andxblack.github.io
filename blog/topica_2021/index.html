Advanced Stochastic Modelling and Monte Carlo methods

Posted on 2021, Jan 14 One min read

In Semester 1 this year, I will again be teaching an honours course which I have roughly titled: Advanced Stochastic Modelling and Monte Carlo methods. For those of you considering it, this post gives an outline of the topics I intend to cover this year. Due to the restrictions on in-person teaching and workload constraints, all honours courses will be run as reading courses. This means there will be no lectures and instead a single weekly workshop. If you want more information please come and see me.

  1. Monte Carlo methods and random number generation.
  2. Path tracing, aka solving the light transport equation.
  3. SDE models and option pricing.
  4. Multi-level Monte Carlo
  5. Continuos-time Markov chain models, reaction network specification.
  6. Markov chain Monte Carlo: Metropolis-Hastings, Gibbs etc….
  7. Inference for state-space models, Kalman filter
  8. Sequential Monte Carlo, particle filters
